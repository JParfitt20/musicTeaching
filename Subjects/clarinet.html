History

The clarinet was invented in 1690 by Johann Christoph Denner and just like the saxophone, it uses a reed to make a sound. He based his instrument on an earlier instrument called the chalumeau. Since then, minor changes have been made to improve playability.

The clarinet is classed a member of the woodwind section of a classical orchestra, but is also used in big-band jazz and even some rock music!

Clarinets come in three main keys and each have their place in an orchestra.

Famous players

There are many well known names in the world of clarinet. Some that you might like to check out are: Woody Allen, Bob Wilbur and Arty Shaw.
