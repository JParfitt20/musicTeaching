History

Although the saxophone is often associated with jazz or pop music, it does in fact have a place in a classical orchestra. There are at least 6 different kinds and sizes of saxophone but the most popular and well recognised is the alto saxophone.

The saxophone was invented in 1840 by Adolphe Sax. It was designed to be like a cross between a brass instrument and a woodwind instrument, although it is classed as a woodwind instrument. Just like the clarinet, the saxophone uses a reed to make the sound and a key mechanism to cover the holes.

Famous players

Many of the better known saxophonists are jazz musicians such as Ben Webster or John Coltrane who is considered one of the most influential jazz saxophonists from the 1950s-1960s.

However, this doesn't mean that the saxophone doens't have a place in more classical music, as Jess Gillam proved by becoming the first person to reach the final of BBC young musician of the year playing the Saxophone.
